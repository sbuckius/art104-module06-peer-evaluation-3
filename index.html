1 Suggestions for the speculative artwork
  • Does the concept feel genuinely speculative, or does it stay too close to familiar territory?
  • What could make it more ambitious, strange, or unexpected?
  • Are there elements that feel underdeveloped or vague?
2 Is the speculative artwork described and linked to the paper's themes?
  • Is the artwork described in enough detail that a reader can picture it clearly?
  • Does the paper explicitly connect it to the three themes, or does it feel like a separate section?
  • Which themes feel well-linked — and which need a stronger bridge to the artwork concept?
  • Does the writing treat the speculative artwork as the culmination of the paper's ideas, or more like an appendix?
3 Suggestions for how to simulate or prototype the idea

Based on the concept, which of the following formats might work well — and how specifically could they be applied to this project? Add your own ideas beyond this list too.

  • Short video or screen recording — 30–90 sec walking through the user experience. Phone video is fine.
  • Animation or GIF — Hand-drawn, After Effects, Canva, or any tool. Must show change over time.
  • Coded interactive — p5.js, HTML/CSS/JS, Processing. Submit link + screenshot.
  • Sound design — 30–90 sec audio representing the artwork's sonic environment. Paired with a still or animation.
  • Narrated storyboard — 3+ drawn panels with a recorded voiceover walking through the experience.
  • Physical demo video — Film yourself interacting with a physical mock-up to show scale, movement, or behavior.
4 Suggestions for making the speculative artwork idea clearer
  • What parts of the concept were confusing or hard to picture?
  • What details are missing that would help a reader fully visualize it?
  • Is the medium, scale, context, or audience clearly defined?
5 Connecting body paragraphs to the speculative artwork
  • Does each body section feel like it's building toward the speculative artwork, or do they feel separate?
  • Which artworks or ideas in the body connect most naturally — and which feel disconnected?
  • What specific bridges or transitions could be added?
6 Suggestions for the introduction
  • Does the opening hook draw you in, or does the paper start too broadly or generically?
  • Is enough background context given to understand the project's focus and stakes?
  • Is the thesis clear, specific, and present early — or vague and buried?
  • Does the introduction set up all three themes and introduce the speculative artwork concept?
7 Suggestions for the conclusion
  • Does the conclusion return to the thesis in a meaningful way, or just repeat it?
  • Does it tie together the three themes and reference the speculative artwork?
  • Does it leave the reader with a sense of why this project matters beyond itself?
  • Does it end abruptly, or does it close with a strong final thought?
8 Grammar, spelling, and logistical improvements
  • Note any patterns in grammar or punctuation errors — don't just list individual mistakes.
  • Are there sentence fragments, run-ons, or unclear phrasing?
  • Comment on formatting, citation style, or logistical requirements (length, structure, headings).
9 Overall suggestions — go deeper, push further
  • What ideas are worth expanding significantly?
  • Where does the project feel thin or rushed?
  • What single area would have the biggest impact if developed more fully before the final submission?
